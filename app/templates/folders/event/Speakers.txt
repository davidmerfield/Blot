# Speakers

### **Meet the Speaker: Dr. Marguerite St. Clair**

{>|} ![Profile](_professor1.jpg)

**Dr. Marguerite St. Clair** is a celebrated cultural historian and professor at the **Hawthorne Institute for Anthropological Studies**. Known for her sharp wit, impeccable taste, and groundbreaking research, St. Clair’s lectures are as much theatrical storytelling as they are academic discourse. Her latest book, *"Footnotes of a Forgotten World,"* has been hailed as "a love letter to the wonders of human eccentricity."

### **About Prof. Laurence Winslow**

{>|} ![Profile](_professor.jpg)

A 19th-century scholar of anthropology and linguistics, **Prof. Laurence Winslow** was a Harvard-educated polymath with an insatiable curiosity for remote cultures. His journals, once dismissed as overly imaginative, have recently been revisited as groundbreaking works of proto-structural anthropology. Winslow’s last known expedition was to the Velisara Islands in 1879, after which he mysteriously vanished. His writings remain a tantalizing blend of ethnographic record and philosophical musing.
