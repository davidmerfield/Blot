About

Blot turns a folder into a website. It's a blogging platform with no interface. Files in­side your blog’s folder be­come blog posts au­to­mat­i­cally. Blot exists so that you can blog with your fa­vorite tools. Use your fa­vorite text-ed­i­tor to write and Blot does the rest.

Why does Blot exist?

I like the simplicity and durability of plain text files. For years I’ve used text files to store my notes. One day I realized that those text files had enough information – in their contents and metadata – to generate a complete blog, without interfering with the structure of my notes or my writing process. So I made Blot.

Why does Blot cost money?

Your subscription pays for the infrastructure which hosts your blog. It also covers the cost of providing support. Most importantly, it means Blot operates in your best interests alone, rather than those of advertisers or investors.

Can I host Blot on my own server?

Yes, Blot’s source code is dedicated to the public domain.

Multiple blogs

You can manage multiple blogs from the same account. Each blog is independent: you could use Dropbox with one and Git with another. Multiple blogs can use the same Dropbox account, or different accounts.

Can I pay annually instead of monthly?

Yes, once you have signed up for Blot you can switch your billing interval from once per month to once per year on the subscription page.

Subscription renewals

If you pay for Blot annually, we will email you one week before your subscription is renewed to give you time to cancel your subscription or update your billing information. If you forget to cancel in time, contact support and we'll send you a refund.

Payment processing

Blot uses Stripe to process subscription payments. This means your payment details do not touch Blot’s servers.

Canceling your subscription

You can cancel your subscription on the account page. You can choose to delete your account immediately, or wait until the end of your billing period.

Bandwidth and storage limits

There is a soft cap of 1TB of bandwidth + storage combined per year, per blog. If you exceed this, we will ask you to meet the cost of hosting your blog, or we will help you set up Blot on your own server. Note that your blog is not affected by Google Drive or Dropbox’s traffic limits because Blot hosts the contents of your folder on its own server.

Language support

Blot’s dashboard, documentation, support and templates are available in English.

How long has Blot been around?

Work on Blot began in the summer of 2013. Blot opened to the public a year later.

How long do you plan to run Blot?

Decades, at least.
